This guide covers the most common issues across the Sprout POS stack and the steps to resolve them at store level. Work through each section in order. If a step doesn't fix the issue, escalate using the contacts in [Section 6](#6-escalation-contacts).

<Note>
  Before troubleshooting anything else, confirm the **Active iPad** is online. It's the source of most POS, kiosk, and docket-printing issues — see [Section 1.3](#13-the-active-ipad-local-server).
</Note>

<AccordionGroup>

### 1.3 The Active iPad (local server)

One iPad in each store acts as the **Active Device** — the local server for your POS network. If the Active iPad goes down, everything else breaks.

<Info>
  **What the Active iPad does**

  - Stores financial transactions and open order data locally.
  - Keeps all other iPads in sync.
  - Sends data to the Lightspeed Back Office.
  - Receives all online orders (Order Anywhere) — these route to the Active Device only.
  - Routes orders to the digital line docket printer (Section 3.2).
</Info>

<Warning>
  **Rules**

  - Only **one** Active Device per store. Multiple Active Devices cause sync errors and instability.
  - The Active iPad **must** stay powered on, connected to Wi-Fi, and have Lightspeed open during all service hours.
  - If the Active iPad disconnects or the app closes, other iPads will stop working properly.
</Warning>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Other iPads not working / orders not syncing">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Suspect the Active iPad first">
        Most likely cause: it's offline or the app has closed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Identify the Active Device">
        Check with your manager or the store setup sheet.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify it's online">
        Powered on, on Wi-Fi, Lightspeed open in foreground.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Reopen the app">
        Wait 1–2 minutes for other iPads to reconnect.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Restart the Active iPad">
        Power off, wait 30 seconds, power on, reopen Lightspeed.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Online orders (Order Anywhere) not coming through">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check the Active Device">
        Online orders route to it only.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify status">
        Online, on Wi-Fi, Lightspeed open.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Allow time after restart">
        Online orders may take a few minutes to resume.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Escalate">
        If still missing after 5 minutes, contact Dave.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How to check or change which iPad is Active">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Back Office">
        POS → Devices.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check status">
        Active or Passive.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Promote a Passive device">
        Takes effect after starting a new shift.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Demote an Active device">
        Takes effect the next day or next shift.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Always confirm with Dave">
        Incorrect setup causes store-wide errors.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Active iPad is damaged or needs replacing">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Promote a replacement">
        Pick another iPad to take over.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Update Back Office">
        POS → Devices → select the replacement → set to Active.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Reassign hardware">
        Assign the printer and payment terminal to the new Active Device.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start a new shift">
        Required for the change to take effect.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Notify Dave">
        For setup review and broken-iPad replacement.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  **Daily check:** At the start of each day, confirm the Active iPad is powered on, connected to Wi-Fi, and has Lightspeed open before opening the store. This prevents most POS and docket-printing issues before they happen.
</Tip>

## 2. Payment terminal issues (Five.ID)

All Sprout stores use Five.ID terminals for card payments and Leat loyalty scanning.

### 2.1 Card reader connection & hardware

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="“Card reader not connected” error">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check the cable">
        Firmly connected at both ends.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Reseat the cable">

      </Step>
      <Step title="Power-cycle the terminal">
        Unplug, wait 15 seconds, replug.
      </Step>

  <Accordion title="Terminal not turning on or repeatedly restarting">

      <Step title="Avoid loop-restarts">

      </Step>

      <Step title="Escalate">

    </Steps>

### 4.2 Router restart procedure

<Warning>
  A router restart will temporarily disconnect **all** devices in the store (POS, Five terminals, kiosks, KDS, printers). Only restart at a natural pause or when the network is already fully down.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the router">
    Usually in the back office or above the ceiling tiles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Unplug power">
    Pull the power cable from the router.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait 60 seconds">
    Use your phone timer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Plug back in">
    Reconnect the power cable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for boot">
    3–5 minutes; lights should stabilise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reconnect devices in order">
    iPad → Five terminal → kiosks → printers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test transactions">
    On both till and kiosk.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### 4.3 Backup payment processing (Square terminals)

Each store has backup Square terminals for use when the primary network and payment systems are down. Square supports an offline mode that allows card payments without an internet connection.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How to switch to Square backup terminals">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Locate the terminal">
        Back office or manager's area.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Power on">
        Should be kept charged and ready.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enable offline mode">
        Settings → Checkout → Offline Mode.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Process payments">
        Inform customers you're using a backup system.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Note the risk">
        Offline payments queue and process when reconnected. Some may be declined later.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Keep a manual log">
        Amount, time, order number for reconciliation.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="When to switch back to the primary system">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Wait for Lightspeed and Five.ID">
        Once the network is restored.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Stop new Square payments">
        Allow queued offline payments to sync.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm sync">
        Check the Square dashboard or app.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Notify Dave">
        For reconciliation against Lightspeed.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  **Offline limitations (even with Square backup)**

  - Kiosks cannot process orders or payments — lock them via the admin panel.
  - Leat loyalty scanning will not work.
  - Delivery platform orders won't come through (Deliveroo, Just Eat, Uber Eats).
  - Take orders manually at the till; call to the kitchen verbally if KDS is also down.
  - Use a mobile hotspot for the primary systems first; Square is a last resort.
  - If the store is offline for more than 2 hours with no resolution in sight, escalate to your Area Manager about closing.
</Warning>

## 5. Kiosk issues (Kurve on Elo I-Series)

Sprout kiosks run Kurve software on Elo I-Series 22" Android touchscreens. The kiosk card machines have a separate Wi-Fi connection and require supervisor code `2013` to access settings.

<Tip>
  **For all kiosk errors:** Take a photo or screenshot of the error and send it to Kurve Support via WhatsApp (`+44 117 325 3256`) with the store name and time. Do this **first**, before troubleshooting, so Kurve can begin investigating.
</Tip>
