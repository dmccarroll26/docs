All Sprout stores run Lightspeed K Series on iPad. Most POS issues trace back to one of three places: the app itself, the till's connection to other devices, or the **Active iPad** (your local server).

<Note>
  Before troubleshooting anything else, confirm the Active iPad is online — it's the source of most POS, kiosk, and docket-printing issues. See [The Active iPad](#the-active-ipad-local-server).
</Note>

## App frozen or unresponsive

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="App is frozen, buttons not responding">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the app switcher">
        Double-tap the iPad Home button, or swipe up and pause on newer iPads.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Force-close Lightspeed">
        Swipe the Lightspeed app up and off the screen.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Reopen the app">
        Wait 10 seconds, then reopen Lightspeed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Restart the iPad">
        If it freezes again within 5 minutes, hold Power + Volume Up until the slider appears.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Escalate">
        If the issue persists after the restart, contact Dave / Tech Support.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="App crashes on launch (closes immediately)">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Restart the iPad">
        Power off, wait 30 seconds, power on.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Update Lightspeed">
        Open the App Store and install any pending updates.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check storage">
        Settings → General → iPad Storage. If less than 1 GB free, delete unused apps.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Reinstall Lightspeed">
        Delete and reinstall the app. Credentials are in the store iPad folder.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Escalate">
        Contact Dave if reinstalling doesn't fix it.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Till not processing orders

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Orders won't send to kitchen / KDS">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check Wi-Fi">
        See [Network & Wi-Fi](/operations/network).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check the KDS">
        Confirm the device is powered on and on the same network.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify the connection">
        In Lightspeed: Settings → Printers & Devices.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Restart the KDS">
        If it shows as disconnected.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Workaround">
        Call orders verbally and log on paper until resolved.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Duplicate orders appearing">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Don't void blindly">
        Duplicates may be legitimate.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check for double taps">
        Common during lag.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Void with a reason">
        Manager → Void with a clear reason note.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Restart Lightspeed">
        If duplicates keep appearing.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Log it">
        Note in the store comms sheet for end-of-day reconciliation.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Menu items missing or showing wrong prices">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Pull to refresh">
        Swipe down on the order screen.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Force a sync">
        Settings → Sync → **Sync Now**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check with Ops">
        Confirm with Dave whether a menu update was pushed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Don't adjust at the till">
        Escalate for a central fix.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The Active iPad (local server)

One iPad in each store acts as the **Active Device** — the local server for your POS network. If the Active iPad goes down, everything else breaks.

<Info>
  **What the Active iPad does**

  - Stores financial transactions and open order data locally.
  - Keeps all other iPads in sync.
  - Sends data to the Lightspeed Back Office.
  - Receives all online orders (Order Anywhere) — these route to the Active Device only.
  - Routes orders to the digital line docket printer.
</Info>

<Warning>
  **Rules**

  - Only **one** Active Device per store. Multiple Active Devices cause sync errors and instability.
  - The Active iPad **must** stay powered on, connected to Wi-Fi, and have Lightspeed open during all service hours.
  - If the Active iPad disconnects or the app closes, other iPads will stop working properly.
</Warning>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Other iPads not working / orders not syncing">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Suspect the Active iPad first">
        Most likely cause: it's offline or the app has closed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Identify the Active Device">
        Check with your manager or the store setup sheet.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify it's online">
        Powered on, on Wi-Fi, Lightspeed open in foreground.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Reopen the app">
        Wait 1–2 minutes for other iPads to reconnect.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Restart the Active iPad">
        Power off, wait 30 seconds, power on, reopen Lightspeed.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Online orders (Order Anywhere) not coming through">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check the Active Device">
        Online orders route to it only.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify status">
        Online, on Wi-Fi, Lightspeed open.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Allow time after restart">
        Online orders may take a few minutes to resume.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Escalate">
        If still missing after 5 minutes, contact Dave.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How to check or change which iPad is Active">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Back Office">
        POS → Devices.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check status">
        Active or Passive.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Promote a Passive device">
        Takes effect after starting a new shift.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Demote an Active device">
        Takes effect the next day or next shift.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Always confirm with Dave">
        Incorrect setup causes store-wide errors.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Active iPad is damaged or needs replacing">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Promote a replacement">
        Pick another iPad to take over.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Update Back Office">
        POS → Devices → select the replacement → set to Active.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Reassign hardware">
        Assign the printer and payment terminal to the new Active Device.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start a new shift">
        Required for the change to take effect.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Notify Dave">
        For setup review and broken-iPad replacement.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  **Daily check:** At the start of each day, confirm the Active iPad is powered on, connected to Wi-Fi, and has Lightspeed open before opening the store. This prevents most POS and docket-printing issues before they happen.
</Tip>
